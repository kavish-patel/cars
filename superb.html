SKODA SUPERB

The Superb is Skoda’s flagship offering in the country. The manufacturer recently added a few features to it as part of its model year update. Priced from Rs 31.99 lakh (ex-showroom), the Superb is a petrol-only offering available in two broad trims: Sportline and Laurin & Klement.

The Skoda Superb has only one competitor in the country, the Toyota Camry hybrid.

The Skoda Superb gets a 2.0-litre TSI turbo-petrol engine that generates 190PS of power and 320Nm of torque. There’s only a single transmission option on offer: a 7-speed DSG dual-clutch automatic gearbox. The Superb is 4,869mm long, 1,864mm wide, 1,469mm tall, and sits on a 2,841mm wheelbase.

The Skoda Superb gets features such as an 8-inch touchscreen infotainment system with wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay, a panoramic sunroof, wireless charging, three-zone climate control, digital instrument display, powered and ventilated front seats, and adaptive LED headlamps with follow-me-home lights. Safety is taken care of by eight airbags, electronic stability control (ESC), tyre pressure monitoring system, hill brake assist, hill hold control, a 360-degree camera with park assist, and front and rear parking sensors.
