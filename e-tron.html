AUDI E-TRON

Audi has launched the e-tron in India from Rs 99.99 lakh (ex-showroom). It is offered in two body styles: Standard (SUV) and Sportback (SUV-coupe) across three variants: e-tron 50, e-tron 55, and e-tron 55 Sportback.

The all-electric Audi comes equipped with two powertrain options and offers a Worldwide Harmonized Light Vehicles Test Procedure (WLTP) range of up to 484km depending on the variant. Audi offers the e-tron with two complimentary home chargers as standard. Additionally, the manufacturer has rolled out several aftersales, warranty, and buyback plans.

The Audi e-tron is priced from Rs 99.99 lakh to Rs 1.18 crore (ex-showroom). It rivals the Mercedes-Benz EQC and Jaguar i-PACE.

The Audi e-tron employs a dual-motor setup, in two different outputs, that drives all four wheels. The base-spec 50 that comes with a 71.2kWh battery pack puts out 312PS and 540Nm. On the other hand, the 55 makes 360PS and 561Nm (up to 408PS and 664Nm in Boost), drawing power from a 95kWh battery pack. The 50 is capable of doing the 0-100kmph sprint in 6.8 seconds, whereas the 55 does the job in 5.7 seconds. In terms of claimed efficiency, the e-tron has a Worldwide Harmonized Light Vehicles Test Procedure (WLTP) range of 379km (50) and 484km (55). The e-tron SUV is 5,014mm long, 1,976mm wide, and 1,686mm tall with a wheelbase of 2,928mm. The e-tron Sportback is 5,014mm long, 1,976mm wide, and 1,673mm tall with a 2,928mm wheelbase.

Safety is covered by eight airbags, ABS with EBD, electronic stability control, tyre pressure monitor, and front and rear parking sensors.
